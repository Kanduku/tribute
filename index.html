Tribute to Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

Early Life

Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam was born on 15th October 1931 in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India. He was the youngest of five siblings and lived in a modest household. His early education was at Schwartz Higher Secondary School in Ramanathapuram. Later, he went on to study physics at St. Joseph's College, Tiruchirappalli, and aerospace engineering at the Madras Institute of Technology (MIT).

Motivational Words

"Dream, dream, dream. Dreams transform into thoughts and thoughts result in action."

"To succeed in your mission, you must have single-minded devotion to your goal."

"Don't take rest after your first victory because if you fail in second, more lips are waiting to say that your first victory was just luck."

Last Day

On 27th July 2015, Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam collapsed while delivering a lecture at the Indian Institute of Management Shillong. He was rushed to the hospital but sadly passed away due to a cardiac arrest. His sudden demise left the entire nation in mourning. Dr. Kalam's legacy continues to inspire millions across the globe.
